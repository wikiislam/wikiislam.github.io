Philosophical issues with Hell

Some argue that Hell is a deterrent to prevent people from disbelief. However, if a person truly disbelieves, a threat from a book that he doesn’t believe in will likely be ineffective.

Torturing the majority of people is a greater evil than whatever benefits these threats provide. A most-merciful God could have encouraged people to do good deeds via rewarding good behavior, without threatening punishment. Even for more serious transgressions, eternal punishment for something committed during a finite human lifetime will strike many as a disproportionately harsh sentence.

The threat of hell is often ineffective as believers are motivated to follow the Quran at all costs but they often end up misinterpreting the text.

Hell is also useful in manipulating people. To get a believer to follow your personal wishes, threaten them with hell if they don’t follow your interpretation of an Islamic position. Perhaps many Hadith and Quranic verses used hell to manipulate people.
