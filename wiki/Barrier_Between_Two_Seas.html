Barrier Between Two Seas

From WikiIslam, the online resource on Islam
Jump to: navigation, search
This article expresses a pro-Islamic viewpoint.

Rivers flow together and there nature came to be known in the start of the 20th century, where as Allah mentioned the nature of these two rivers in the Holy Qur'an around 1400 years ago.

These two river flow together and finally settle down in the Ocean. The taste of one river is extremely sweet whereas the taste of other river is the opposite (that is bitter) but they never mix. There is nothing in between these rivers which stops them from mixing together but it's is the will and order of the Almighty Allah.

فَبِأَىِّ ءَالَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ (﻿١٨﻿)

مَرَجَ ٱلۡبَحۡرَيۡنِ يَلۡتَقِيَانِ (﻿١٩﻿)

بَيۡنَہُمَا بَرۡزَخٌ۬ لَّا يَبۡغِيَانِ (﻿٢٠﻿)


Ar-Rahman

In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful

Lord of the two Easts, and Lord of the two Wests! (17)

Which is it, of the favours of your Lord, that ye deny? (18)

He hath loosed the two seas. They meet. (19)

There is a barrier between them. They encroach not (one upon the other). (20)
