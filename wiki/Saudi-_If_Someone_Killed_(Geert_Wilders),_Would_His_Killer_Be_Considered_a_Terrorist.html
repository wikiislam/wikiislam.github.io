Translated from the original Arabic by Al Mutarjim

This is a short article from Saudi paper al-Riyadh, published on March 11, 2010. It is an article about Geert Wilders, "the racist" as he is universally called in Arabic newspapers, discussing his planned sequel to Fitna. This one provoked dozens of reader comments. Most of the comments read fell into three broad categories: those calling for jihad, those calling on Muslims to cut off relations with Western countries that support Wilders, and those calling for Muslims to make similar films.

Amsterdam: Dutch rightwing lawmaker Geert Wilders, known for his racist comments against Islam, announced his intent to show a new film which contradicts the Islamic religion, after the next parliamentary elections scheduled for the 9th of June.

Wilders, who leads the rightist Freedom Party, said yesterday: “The film will be provocative, but we will not do it until after the elections.”

Wilders produced the first part of his film “Fitna” on the internet in March of 2008.

In his new film, Wilders intended to tackle the issue of “the massive emigration from Islamic countries” to Europe, as well as what he described as the “Islamization of Europe.”

Wilders remarked that “Fitna 2” will also deal with the issue of freedom of opinion and Islamic shari’ah. Wilders said that he obtained professional assistance from the United States to produce the new film.

It should be noted that the first part of the film “Fitna” provoked strong protests in Islamic countries.
